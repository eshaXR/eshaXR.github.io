🚶 Safety Research

VRU Personal Listening Device Study

This study investigated whether the type of headphones a pedestrian wears affects how quickly they react to an oncoming vehicle alert. Participants crossed a virtual street while wearing open-ear, closed-ear, or bone-conduction headphones and responded to verbal and non-verbal car alerts. Open-ear headphones led to the fastest reaction times, and verbal alerts were more effective than tones.

🎧 Psychophysics

Audio-Visual Order Threshold (AOT/PSE) Experiment

This experiment measures how much a sound and a visual event can be out of sync before a person notices they are not happening at the same time. Participants wear a Meta Quest 3 AR headset and respond to paired audio and visual stimuli while the time gap between them is adjusted using a staircase method. The results reveal how the brain determines which event came first and how well it tolerates small timing differences.

💊 UX Design

Medication Adherence for Blood Pressure

A Figma interface concept that reimagines how patients track blood pressure medication over time. Instead of tables or graphs, readings are displayed as radial clock visualizations that make it easy to spot patterns and concerning trends at a glance. The design surfaces alerts like elevated sodium intake or blood pressure spikes directly alongside the timeline view.

📡 Assistive Tech

Navibar: Vibrotactile Navigation for PMV Users

Navibar is a navigation system that uses vibrations on the body to guide personal mobility vehicle users, replacing or supplementing traditional visual and audio cues. The research explored whether people could accurately interpret directional information delivered through touch alone. Published in the 2023 HFES Annual Meeting proceedings.
